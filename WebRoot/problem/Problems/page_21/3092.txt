</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Every positive integer <i><b>N</b></i> can be written in at least one way as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>) where no term in the sum exactly divides any other term in the sum. For example:</p><blockquote><p>1 = (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>)<br>7 = (2<sup>2</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>) + (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>1</sup>)<br>31 = (2<sup>4</sup>)(3<sup>0</sup>) + (2<sup>0</sup>)(3<sup>2</sup>) + (2<sup>1</sup>)(3<sup>1</sup>) = (2<sup>2</sup>) + (3<sup>3</sup>)</p></blockquote><p>Note from the example of 31 that the representation is not unique.</p><p>Write a program which takes as input a positive integer <i><b>N</b></i> and outputs a representation of <i><b>N</b></i> as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>).</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>The first line of input contains a single integer <i><b>C</b></i> (1 &le; <i><b>C</b></i> &le; 1000) which is the number of datasets that follow.</p><p>Each dataset consists of a single line of input containing a single integer <i><b>N</b></i> (1 &le; <i><b>N</b></i> &lt; 2<sup>31</sup>), which is the number to be represented as a sum of terms of the form (2<sup>a</sup>)(3<sup>b</sup>).</p></span></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>For each dataset, the output will be a single line consisting of: The dataset number, a single space, the number of terms in your sum as a decimal integer followed by a single space followed by representations of the terms in the form <code>[&lt;2 exponent&gt;,&lt;3 exponent&gt;]</code> with terms separated by a single space. <code>&lt;2 exponent&gt;</code> is the power of 2 in the term and <code>&lt;3 exponent&gt;</code> is the power of 3 in the term.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">6
