</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Farmer John has a pig farm near town A. He wants to visit his friend living in town B. During this journey he will visit <i>n</i> small villages so he decided to earn some money. He tooks <i>n</i> pigs and plans to sell one pig in each village he visits.</p><p>Pork prices in villages are different, in the <i>j</i>-th village the people would buy a pork at <i>p<sub>j</sub></i> rubles per kilogram. The distance from town A to the <i>j</i>-th village along the road to town B is <i>d<sub>j</sub></i> kilometers.</p><p>Pigs have different weights. Transporting one kilogram of pork per one kilometer of the road needs <i>t</i> rubles for addition fuel.</p><p>Help John decide, which pig to sell in each town in order to earn as much money as possible.</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>The first line of the input file contains integer numbers <i>n</i> (1 ≤ <i>n</i> ≤ 1000) and <i>t</i> (1 ≤ <i>t</i> ≤ 10<sup>9</sup>). The second line contains <i>n</i> integer numbers <i>w<sub>i</sub></i> (1 ≤ <i>w<sub>i</sub></i> ≤ 10<sup>9</sup>) — the weights of the pigs. The third line contains <i>n</i> integer numbers <i>d<sub>j</sub></i> (1 ≤ <i>d<sub>j</sub></i> ≤ 10<sup>9</sup>) — the distances to the villages from the town A. The fourth line contains <i>n</i> integer numbers <i>p<sub>j</sub></i> (1 ≤ <i>p<sub>j</sub></i> ≤ 10<sup>9</sup>) — the prices of pork in the villages.</p></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Output <i>n</i> numbers, the <i>j</i>-th number is the number of pig to sell in the <i>j</i>-th village. The pigs are numbered from 1 in the order they are listed in the input file.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">3 1
10 20 15
10 20 30
50 70 60</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">3 2 1</pre><p class="pst">
