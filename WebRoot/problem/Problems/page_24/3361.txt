</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Let <i>a</i>, <i>b</i>, <i>c</i>, <i>d</i> be integers. The complex number <i>a</i>+<i>bj</i>, where <i>j</i><sup>2</sup> = -1, is a factor of <i>c</i>+<i>dj</i>, if there exist integers <i>e</i> and <i>f</i> such that</p><p align="center"><i>c</i> + <i>dj</i> = (<i>a</i> + <i>bj</i>)(<i>e</i> + <i>fj</i>).</p><p>A complex number <i>a</i> + <i>bj</i> where <i>a</i> and <i>b</i> are integers is a Gaussian prime if the factors are 1, -1, -<i>a</i> - <i>bj</i> and <i>a</i> + <i>bj</i> only.</p><p>The following are Gaussian primes: 1 + <i>j</i>, 1 - <i>j</i>, 1 + 2<i>j</i>, 1 - 2<i>j</i>, 3 and 7.</p><p>The Gaussian prime factors of 5 are:</p><p><blockquote>1 + 2<i>j</i> and 1 - 2<i>j</i>, or<br>2 + <i>j</i> and 2 - <i>j</i>, or<br>-1 - 2<i>j</i> and -1 + 2<i>j</i>, or<br>-2 - <i>j</i> and -2 + <i>j</i>.</blockquote></p><p>Write a program that finds all the Gaussian prime factors of a positive integer.</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>One line of input per case. The line represents a positive integer <i>n</i>.</p></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">One line of output per test case. The line represents the Gaussian prime factors of <i>n</i>. If <i>a</i> + <i>bj</i> is a Gaussian prime factor of <i>n</i>, then <i>a</i> &gt; 0, |<i>b</i>| ≥ <i>a</i>, if <i>b</i> ≠ 0. If <i>b</i> = 0, the output must be <i>a</i>.
<br></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">2
5
6
700</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">Case #1: 1+j, 1-j 
Case #2: 1+2j, 1-2j

Case #4: 1+j, 1-j, 1+2j, 1-2j, 7</pre><p class="pst">Hint</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>Output the Gaussian prime factors in ascending order of <i>a</i>. If there are more than one factors with the same <i>a</i>, output them in ascending order of <i>b</i> by absolute value.
