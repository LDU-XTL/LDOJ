</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>In a fictitious solar system consisting of star S, a planet P, and its moon M, planet P orbits in a perfect circle around star S with a revolution period of exactly <i>T</i> Earth days, and moon M orbits in a perfect circle around planet P with an unknown revolution period. Given the position of moon M relative to star S at three different time points, your goal is to compute the distance of planet P from star S.</p><p>To do this, consider a two-dimensional Cartesian coordinate system with its origin centered at star S. You may assume that P&rsquo;s counterclockwise orbit around S and M&rsquo;s counterclockwise orbit around P both lie completely within the <i>x</i> &ndash; <i>y</i> coordinate plane. Let (<i>x</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>y</i><sub>1</sub>) denote the position of the moon M on the first observation, let (<i>x</i><sub>2</sub>, <i>y</i><sub>2</sub>) denote its position <i>k</i><sub>1</sub> Earth days later, and let (<i>x</i><sub>3</sub>, <i>y</i><sub>3</sub>) denote its position <i>k</i><sub>2</sub> Earth days after the second observation.</p></span></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><span lang="en-us"><p>The input test file will contain multiple test cases. Each test case consists of two lines. The first line contains the integers <i>T</i>, <i>k</i><sub>1</sub>, and <i>k</i><sub>2</sub>, where 1 &le; <i>T</i>, <i>k</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>k</i><sub>2</sub> &le; 1000. The second line contains six floating-point values <i>x</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>y</i><sub>1</sub>, <i>x</i><sub>2</sub>, <i>y</i><sub>2</sub>, <i>x</i><sub>3</sub>, and <i>y</i><sub>3</sub>. Input points have been selected so as to guarantee a unique solution; the final distance from planet P to star S will always be within 0.1 of the nearest integer. The end-of-file is denoted with a single line containing &ldquo;0 0 0&rdquo;.</p></span></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>For each input case, the program should print the distance from planet P to star S, rounded to the nearest integer.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">360 90 90
