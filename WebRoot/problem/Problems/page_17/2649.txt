</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The factorial function, n! is defined thus for n a non-negative integer: 
<br><pre>   0! = 1
<br>   n! = n * (n-1)!   (n > 0)</pre>
<br>We say that a divides b if there exists an integer k such that 
<br><pre>   k*a = b</pre>
<br>
<br></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">The input to your program consists of several lines, each containing two non-negative integers, n and m, both less than 2^31.</div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US">For each input line, output a line stating whether or not m divides n!, in the format shown below.</div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">6 9
6 27
20 10000
20 100000
1000 1009
</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">9 divides 6!
27 does not divide 6!
10000 divides 20!
100000 does not divide 20!
1009 does not divide 1000!
