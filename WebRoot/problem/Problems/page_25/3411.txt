</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>A network of <b>m</b> roads connects <b>N</b> cities (numbered from 1 to <b>N</b>). There may be more than one road connecting one city with another. Some of the roads are paid. There are two ways to pay for travel on a paid road <b>i</b> from city <b>a<sub>i</sub></b> to city <b>b<sub>i</sub></b>:</p><ul><li>in advance, in a city <b>c<sub>i</sub></b> (which may or may not be the same as <b>a<sub>i</sub></b>);</li><li>after the travel, in the city <b>b<sub>i</sub></b>.</li></ul><p>The payment is <b>P<sub>i</sub></b> in the first case and <b>R<sub>i</sub></b> in the second case.</p><p>Write a program to find a minimal-cost route from the city 1 to the city <b>N</b>.</p></div><p class="pst">Input</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>The first line of the input contains the values of <b>N</b> and <b>m</b>. Each of the following <b>m</b> lines describes one road by specifying the values of <b>a<sub>i</sub></b>, <b>b<sub>i</sub></b>, <b>c<sub>i</sub></b>, <b>P<sub>i</sub></b>, <b>R<sub>i</sub></b> (1 &#8804; <b>i </b>&#8804; <b>m</b>). Adjacent values on the same line are separated by one or more spaces. All values are integers, 1 &#8804; <b>m, N</b> &#8804; 10, 0 &#8804; <b>P<sub>i</sub></b> , <b>R<sub>i</sub></b> &#8804; 100, <b>P</b><sub>i</sub> &#8804; <b>R<sub>i</sub></b> (1 &#8804; <b>i </b>&#8804; <b>m</b>).</p></div><p class="pst">Output</p><div class="ptx" lang="en-US"><p>The first and only line of the file must contain the minimal possible cost of a trip from the city 1 to the city <b>N</b>. If the trip is not possible for any reason, the line must contain the word &#8216;<b>impossible</b>&#8217;.</p></div><p class="pst">Sample Input</p><pre class="sio">4 5
1 2 1 10 10
2 3 1 30 50
3 4 3 80 80
2 1 2 10 10
1 3 2 10 50</pre><p class="pst">Sample Output</p><pre class="sio">110</pre><p class="pst">
